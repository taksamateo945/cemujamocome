Lily Gladstone Wiki, Biography, and Background

Lily Gladstone was born on August 2, 1986, and grew up in Browning, Montana. Her father, a broadcast journalist, is of Blackfeet and Nimíipuu origin, and her mother, an early childhood education specialist, is white. Gladstone resided on the Blackfeet Nation reservation until the age of eleven. For her first few years, the family lived in a log cabin with a wood-burning stove.

Lily Gladstone is an American performer of Blackfeet and Nimíipuu descent. She is best recognized for her role in Martin Scorsese’s 2023 film Killers of the Flower Moon, in which she plays an Osage woman living on an Oklahoma reservation during a string of killings in the 1920s.

Following her family’s relocation from Browning owing to insufficient economic prospects, she completed her high school education in a Seattle suburb before enrolling at the University of Montana.

2008 saw her get a bachelor’s degree in fine arts with a minor in Native American studies, after studying acting and theatre. She participated in undergraduate productions of Montana Repertory Theatre, where she received the following praise from director and instructor Greg Johnson:

“There are a lot of talented kids at UM, but she’s always been a standout. She’s absolutely a transcendent actress. We were lucky to have her.”
