Who is Ryan Coogler’s wife? Zinzi Evans Wiki Bio

Zinzi Evans was born in Oakland, California USA, on 18 April 1985, so under the Zodiac sign of Pisces, and of American nationality. She is a producer, assistant manager, and an assistant director who came into the spotlight after marrying Ryan Coogler, a movie director who directed the popular “Black Panther” movie in 2018.

Early life and education

Zinzi grew up in California alongside her father Zishan, her mother Maria, and with five sisters and a brother. She was physically active while at high school, participating in cross-country racing – she was a letterwoman who later became a team captain, and won seven league championships.

She is considered to be amongst the best Fresno Bulldog team’s cross-country runners – she studied at California State University, Fresno, and graduated with a Bachelor of Arts degree in communicative disorders and deaf studies in 2007.

Career as an assistant director and other works

Zinzi has served as an assistant director and producer for numerous TV commercials and short movies, while she also helped her husband with the popular “Black Panther” movie released in 2018, and as a consultant in films such as “Rampage” and “A Wrinkle in Time” both released in the same year.

Some of her other works include being a sign language consultant during the making of the “Creed” movie in 2015, and serving as the “Locks” movie’s assistant director and producer.

Marriage with Ryan Coogler

Zinzi and Evans met for the first time in Oakland, California – he was born and raised there too – they officially began dating in 2012, and married years later in May 2016. Evans explained how his wife helped him with his career while they were still dating, as he was writing his screenplays in Microsoft Word before she bought the Final Draft software for writing movies for him.

Zinzi is expected to give birth to their first child in late 2019, as she announced her pregnancy on 24 February 2019, during the 91st Academy Awards event she and Ryan attended together. Ryan expressed his desire for having a daughter, when he said in an interview ‘I found I want daughters real, real bad’.

Who is Ryan Coogler?

Ryan Kyle Coogler was born on 23 May 1986 – his birth sign is Gemini and he holds American nationality. Ryan is a film director, screenwriter, and producer, who became a star after his movie “Black Panther” became a hit and broke multiple box office records – it is the highest grossing movie of all times to be directed by a black person.

Ryan was raised with his two brothers – Keenan and Noah – by their father Ira, who was a juvenile hall probation counselor, and mother Joselyn, a community organizer; both are graduates of the California State University in Hayward. Clarence Thomas, Ryan’s uncle, served as the secretary treasurer of the International Longshore and Warehouse Union. Ryan ran track and played football at Saint Mary’s College High School in Berkeley, California, and was excellent at mathematics and science – after matriculation, he began studying at Saint Mary’s College of California on a football scholarship. While at college, Ryan was asked by a professor to try creative writing, the same professor who went on to praise his work and advise him to become a screenwriter.

He transferred to Sacramento State University in March 2004, where he continued to play football, and after graduation, enrolled at the USC School of Cinematic Arts, where he wrote his first short movies. Three of the first four films Ryan created were nominated for awards, and won several for the movies “Locks”, “Fig”, and “Gap” released in 2009, 2011, and 2011 respectively. His debut full-length movie entitled “Fruitvale Station” was released in 2013, and follows the story of Oscar Grant, an African-American man who was shot dead at the Fruitvale BART station by a police officer on 1 January 2009.

The movie won numerous awards, and led to Metro Goldwyn Mayer Studios Inc. inviting Ryan to direct the “Creed” sports drama film. The movie was released on 25 November 2015, and after being praised for his skills, Ryan won the Best Director Award from the African-American Film Critics Association, as well as the New Generation Award given by the Los Angeles Film Critics Association. What made Ryan popular worldwide was directing the “Black Panther” film, making him the youngest filmmaker ever to work at the Marvel Studio.
